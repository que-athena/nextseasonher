The Invisible

You disappeared a long time ago, and you're not sure anyone noticed.

One

What you already know.

You are doing everything. At home. At work. For the people who depend on you. And somehow you have become the person no one sees.

Not unloved. Not unwanted. Unseen.

The harder part is quieter. You have started to wonder if there's still a you underneath the roles to be seen. Whether you would recognize her if she walked into the room.

You made yourself useful for so long that you made yourself invisible. The two were not supposed to come together. They did.

Two

This is the season of Recalibration.

Recalibration is what happens when the identity you've been living inside has worn so thin you can no longer locate yourself in it.

Invisibility accumulated. You said yes one too many times. You shrank into the spaces other people made room for. You became indispensable in ways that quietly erased you.

And the woman you were before all of it — the one who took up space, who had preferences, who knew what she wanted — went quiet.

She is not gone. She is underneath.

The visibility you've been craving has been pointed at the wrong audience. No amount of external recognition will reach a woman who can't locate herself.

The people around you cannot see her because you cannot see her. That is where this season begins.

Three

What this actually asks of you.

The work is finding her. The woman underneath the invisibility. The one who has been there the whole time, watching from inside the life you built around her.

Once you see her, the rest of your life starts to rearrange itself around what she actually needs.

Not because you announced it. Because something in the room has changed.
