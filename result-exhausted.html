Two

This is the season of Reorientation.

Burnout is what they call it when they don't know what else to name it. What you're in is more specific.

You built an identity around being the woman who shows up. Who handles it. Who delivers. Who doesn't need much.

That identity worked for a long time. It earned you a life. A role. Respect. It also quietly retired the version of you who used to want things for herself.

You are tired because you have been performing an identity that demands depletion to keep working.

The performance is what's depleting you. Not the workload.

This identity has expired. It is still running you. That's the gap.

The woman who has been carrying everything. The conditioning that built her. What she's been protecting. What she's been costing you. The deal she made with belonging that you don't want to be in anymore.

You see her clearly. Then you stop being her.
